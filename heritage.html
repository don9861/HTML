A World Heritage Site: Pashupatinath Temple

The Pashupatinath Temple is a revered Hindu temple located in the Kathmandu Valley of Nepal. It is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is considered one of the most sacred temples in the world. The temple complex is sprawling and includes many smaller shrines and temples, as well as a cremation site along the Bagmati River. The temple attracts thousands of pilgrims and tourists each year, who come to pay their respects and witness the rich cultural and religious traditions of Nepal. The Pashupatinath Temple is a symbol of the deep spiritual and cultural heritage of Nepal and is a testament to the enduring power of faith and devotion.

Features of Pashupatinath Temple

  1. Pashupatinath temple is a two-storied pagoda-style temple. Its two roofs are made out of copper and plated with gold.
  2. It is a cubic construction with four main doors, all covered with silver sheets.
  3. It is located on the Western bank of Bagmati and is considered a masterpiece of Hindu architecture.
  4. Along the right bank of Bagmati numerous platforms for funeral pyres are built. The cremations on these platforms are a common activity.
